Missouri Old-Time Fiddle Music

Introduction Heritage Influential Musicians Further Discovery Support the Legacy

Late 1700's

  • 1771 - First documented occurrence of the existence of fiddle music among the earliest settlement period in the Ozarks.

Early 1800's

  • Fiddle competitions were held as part of town fairs and picnics in the Ozarks.
  • 1804 to 1806 - The fiddle went west with the Louis and Clark expedition and helped Missouri gain the name “the Mother of the West”.

Mid 1800's

  • Fiddle music in Missouri was influenced by new music such as ragtime and jazz.
  • Growth of stage shows led to the exploitation of the hillbilly image; this was based upon lyrics from the song “Arkansas Traveler”.

Late 1800's

  • 1861 – The fiddle was commonplace amongst troops during the Civil War and countless songs were composed.
  • 1870s – Railroad expansion carried fiddle music to the west and people to Missouri.

Early 1900's

  • Fiddle music was opposed in many church-oriented communities, the terms “Devil’s box” and “Satan’s tool” were coined in reference to the fiddle.
  • 1920s – Introduction of radio programs and 78-rpm recordings led to an interest in learning and performing fiddle music.

Mid 1900's

  • The hillbilly stereotype continued to gain popularity with the comic strip “Li’l Abner” and the television show “Beverly Hillbillies”.
  • 1950s – KWTO radio program Corns-A-Crackin became a nationally syndicated live television show called The Ozarks Jubilee.
  • 1953 – The Ozark Opry opened in Osage Beach, Missouri.
  • 1959 – The Baldnobbers opened in Branson, Missouri.
  • 1960 – Silver Dollar City opened in Branson, Missouri.

Late 1900's

  • 1973 – Ozark Folk Center opened in Mountain View, Arkansas highlighting and supporting Ozarks folk music.
  • 1974 - The Missouri Fiddlers and Country Music Association was founded to preserve and promote Old-Time fiddle and country music.
  • 1979 – the Missouri Old-Time Fiddle Association was founded with the goal of reviving the Missouri State Fiddle Championship.
  • 1987 – Governor Jay Ashcroft signed a decree to make the fiddle the official musical instrument of Missouri.
