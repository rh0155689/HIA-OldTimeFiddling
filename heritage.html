Fiddling Heritage

  • Much of Missouri fiddle music came from old-stock Americans – British Isles based people such as: Scotch Irish (Ulster Scots), Welsh, English and Scots.
  • Even before Missouri was unionized, those of French lineage contributed to Missouri fiddle music with bouillons, cotillions, and the Guillonnée.
  • African American people have also made enormous contributions to old-time fiddle music with minstrel shows, jazz, blues and swing music.
  • German speaking immigrants brought the schottische, varsouvienne, and the polka.
  • American Indians and those with American Indian ancestry blended their native music varieties with fiddle music.
  • Irish railroad workers performed in minstrel shows, vaudeville and participated in the early recording industry.
